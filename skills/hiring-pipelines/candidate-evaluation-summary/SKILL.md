# Candidate Evaluation Summary

Synthesize all interview feedback into one coherent, evidence-based evaluation — every strength and concern tied to an observation — that supports the hiring decision and stands up in the candidate's file.

## Inputs to gather

Gather these before writing. If any are missing, ask for them in a single batched question — never invent feedback, ratings, or interview observations. Mark anything genuinely unavailable as **Unknown** in the output.

- **Candidate** — name
- **Role and target level** — what they applied for
- **Interview feedback** — all interviewer notes (can be unstructured) and ratings, by stage
- **Interview dates** — if available

## Steps

1. Read all feedback before writing. Tie every claim to a specific interview observation; if a claim has no supporting evidence, drop it or mark the evidence **Unknown** — do not infer.
2. Analyze across interviewers: identify consistent signals (2+ interviewers agree), weight evidence by interview type for the skill in question (technical interview > cultural for technical depth), surface contradictions and state which signal is stronger and why, and distinguish "didn't demonstrate" from "demonstrated a weakness."
3. Write the **candidate snapshot**: name, role, interview dates (or **Unknown**), and overall recommendation.
4. Extract **3-5 strengths**, each with the evidence and the interviewer/stage it came from, and a signal-strength label where the feedback supports it (e.g. "Strong signal"). Generic praise needs a concrete example ("explained a complex system clearly without jargon"), not an adjective.
5. Extract **2-3 concerns**, each with evidence and a **severity** assessment — minor (coachable), moderate (needs a plan), major (blocker) — and distinguish a real skill gap from a bad-interview artifact (nerves, a poor question, an off day).
6. Write the **level assessment**: does the candidate meet the bar for the target level? Map evidence to level expectations; note absence of higher-level signals where relevant and whether that's expected.
7. Write **team fit**: how they'd complement the existing team and which gaps they fill — concretely, not as personality praise.
8. Write the **recommendation**: hire/no-hire, confidence level, and any conditions. For a borderline call, be explicit about what tips the balance ("despite concern X, hire because Y"). If one interviewer is a strong no, dig into the specifics — one strong signal may outweigh several weak positives.
9. Adapt to context: apply the **same evidence rigor to internal candidates** — no softer bar because you know them; keep the document **factual and professional** (HR, legal, or the candidate may read it in some jurisdictions); for a **rejected candidate who might reapply**, note specific growth areas and what to look for next time.
10. Assemble the output in the format below.

## Output format

```
**Candidate Evaluation: [Name] — [Role]**

**Recommendation: [Hire / No Hire] ([Confidence: High/Medium/Low])**

**Strengths**
- **[Strength] ([signal strength]):** [Evidence, with interviewer/stage and quote if available.]

**Concerns**
- **[Concern] ([Minor/Moderate/Major]):** [Evidence.] Severity: [why, coachable or not].

**Level Assessment**
[Does the candidate meet the target-level bar? Evidence mapped to level expectations.]

**Team Fit**
[How they complement the team; which gap they fill; collaboration style.]

**Conditions:** [None — proceed to offer, or specific conditions before proceeding.]
```

## Boundaries

- Never state a strength or concern without an interview observation behind it — unsupported claims are dropped or marked **Unknown**.
- Never fabricate ratings, quotes, dates, or interviewer names.
- Never apply a softer standard to internal or known candidates.
- Never assess interviewer calibration or verify claimed experience yourself — evaluate only demonstrated ability in the interviews; the user knows which interviewers run harsh or lenient.
- Never include protected-characteristic or personal commentary; this document may be read by HR, legal, or the candidate, and compliance varies by jurisdiction.

## Chaining

- After a hire recommendation, offer **offer-justification** to justify level and comp to HR/compensation review.
- This summary is typically the input to the debrief's documented decision — if the decision meeting hasn't happened, run **interview-debrief-facilitation** first.
