# Layoff Communication

Draft the communications for a workforce reduction — to the affected individual and to the team that remains — being humane, honest, and clear. Get to the point; do not bury the news; do not use corporate euphemisms.

## Inputs to gather

Gather these before drafting. If any are missing, ask in a single batched question — never invent severance terms, dates, or the reason. Use clear placeholders and mark anything genuinely unavailable as **Unknown**.

- **Number affected on your team** — how many people
- **Reason you can share** — e.g. company-wide cost reduction, org restructure, role elimination (confirm it is not performance-related)
- **Severance** — details, or "HR will follow up"
- **Last day** — the date
- **What's happening to the work** — paused, redistributed, handed off

## Steps

1. Confirm scope: this is for a **non-performance** reduction. If it's performance-related, stop and direct the user to pip-documentation / difficult-conversation-prep with HR — the framing is different.
2. Draft the **Individual Notification** (spoken, for the 1:1): get to the news within ~30 seconds; state clearly the decision is final and not about their performance; cover last day, severance (placeholders), references, equipment return; then explicitly leave space for their reaction — it is not a monologue.
3. Draft the **Team Message** (written, sent only after all notifications are complete): acknowledge what happened directly without euphemism; be honest about what you can and can't share (package details are private); acknowledge it's hard without over-dramatising; state what comes next for the team.
4. Strip all corporate euphemism ("right-sizing", "restructuring", "difficult decision", "let go") in favour of plain, human language.
5. Adapt to context: for a **large-scale layoff (10%+)**, expect a company-wide message first — coordinate timing with the People team; your role is the human follow-up, not the announcement. For **remote teams**, the individual conversation is still video, never written. Reinforce that all communications must go through HR/Legal before use, as the law on what can be said varies by jurisdiction.
6. Assemble the output in the format below.

## Output format

```
**Individual notification — talking points**

*[Sit down. Get to it within 30 seconds.]*

"[Direct statement of the news, that it's final and not performance-related.
Last day, severance (placeholder), references, equipment return.
Genuine, non-performative appreciation.]"

*[Let them respond. Don't fill the silence. Answer what you can; be honest about what you don't know.]*

---

**Team message — to be sent after all individuals have been notified**

[Direct acknowledgement of what happened, no euphemism.
What you can share (it was not performance). What you can't (private package details).
Acknowledge it's hard. What comes next for the team. Open door.]

[Name]
```

## Boundaries

- **Run all communications through HR/Legal before use.** Laws on what can and cannot be said vary significantly by jurisdiction.
- Never invent severance terms, dates, package details, or the reason — use clear placeholders and mark unknowns as **Unknown**.
- Never use this for a performance-related separation; that requires different framing and HR involvement.
- Never deliver a layoff by message — the individual conversation is in person or video. A written notification is not acceptable.
- Never euphemise, over-promise references or outcomes you can't guarantee, or disclose another person's package details. Keep the affected person's dignity central.

## Chaining

- No natural successor — this is a terminal, sensitive artifact. Afterward, supporting the remaining team is ongoing work best handled through **one-on-one-prep** and, if morale needs it, **team-health-survey-analysis**.
