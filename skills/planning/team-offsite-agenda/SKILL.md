# Team Offsite Agenda

Design an offsite that is substantive and energising, not forced activities people resent — aimed at the things that are genuinely hard to get day-to-day: alignment, decisions, and connection.

## Inputs to gather

Gather these before designing. If any are missing, ask for them in a single batched question — never invent the team's goal, topics, or tensions. Mark anything genuinely unavailable as **Unknown**.

- **Team** — name, headcount, and day-to-day mode (remote / hybrid / in-person)
- **Duration** — half-day / full-day / multi-day
- **Primary goal** — the one thing the offsite must achieve above all else
- **Topics to cover** — the list
- **Known tensions or dynamics** — anything to handle carefully (optional but valuable)

## Steps

1. Build a timed **session schedule**. For each session give: duration, session name, format (workshop / discussion / decision / social / working session), facilitator, and desired output (what should exist or be decided by the end).
2. Apply the design principles: open with something that gets people talking (not a slide deck); place the hardest or most contentious topic in the morning when energy is highest; build in genuine breaks (real recovery time, not 10 minutes); end with clarity on next steps and owners, not more discussion.
3. Ensure every session has a concrete desired output — a session without one is a meeting, not an offsite block.
4. Pick the single most important session and write a **3–5 bullet facilitation guide** for it — how to open, how to structure the discussion or decision, and how to land an outcome (e.g. score options against agreed criteria to externalise disagreement from people onto options; if still split, the leader decides and explains why, documented immediately).
5. Adapt to context: multi-day — Day 1 strategy/alignment, Day 2 execution planning and team time; don't mix them. Fully remote — a 4-hour virtual offsite beats a full day of Zoom fatigue; focus on decisions only. High-tension dynamics — bring an external facilitator for the contentious session so the leader can participate rather than referee. Half-day — pick one primary goal only; one good decision beats four inconclusive discussions.
6. Assemble the output in the format below.

## Output format

```
**[Team] Offsite — [Duration]**

| Time | Session | Format | Facilitator | Output |
|------|---------|--------|-------------|--------|
| [9:00] | [name] | [Discussion/Decision/Workshop/Working/Social] | [who or Unknown] | [what's decided/produced] |
| [time] | Break | — | — | — |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

---

**Facilitation guide: [most important session] ([duration])**
- [How to open]
- [How to structure the discussion/decision]
- [How to land an outcome and document it]
```

## Boundaries

- Never invent the primary goal, topic list, or team tensions — mark them **Unknown**.
- Never schedule a session without a concrete desired output.
- Never put the hardest decision in the afternoon, skip real breaks, or end on open discussion rather than owners and next steps.
- Never paper over a known tension with a social activity — name it and design a session that resolves it.
